Grant funding

ECO4 and GBIS, in plain English.

Two government-backed schemes fund external wall insulation for qualifying London households. Most pay only a fraction of the headline cost. Some pay nothing at all.

How the funding works

Both schemes are paid for by the major UK energy companies as part of their government obligations to fund household energy efficiency improvements. The funding is paid directly from the energy company to us, applied against your invoice.

You never handle the grant money. You don't apply for it yourself. Your invoice shows the grant amount applied and your net contribution. The figure you see on your quote is the figure you pay.

The two schemes covering EWI:

ECO4 (Energy Company Obligation, version 4) targets lower-income and vulnerable households and homes with the worst energy efficiency ratings. Typical grants £6,000 to £12,000 toward EWI.

The Great British Insulation Scheme (GBIS) has much broader eligibility based on Council Tax band and EPC rating, with no income test. Typical grants £4,000 to £8,000.

Many London households qualify for at least partial funding under one or both schemes. Our survey confirms exactly what you're entitled to.

Typical grant amounts

  • Terraced (2-3 bed)£6,000 – £10,000
  • Semi-detached£7,000 – £11,000
  • End-of-terrace£7,500 – £11,500
  • Detached property£8,000 – £12,000
Exact amount depends on property type, EPC rating, household circumstances, and the obligated energy supplier. Your survey provides the precise figure.
ECO4 in detail

ECO4. Higher grant amounts, narrower eligibility.

If you qualify for ECO4, the funding typically covers most or all of the installation cost.

You may qualify for ECO4 if:

  • Anyone in your household receives qualifying benefits (Universal Credit, Pension Credit, Income Support, ESA, JSA, Child Tax Credit, Working Tax Credit, Housing Benefit)
  • Your property has an EPC rating of D, E, F or G
  • You're referred via LA Flex (your local authority can flex eligibility for fuel-poor households outside benefits criteria)
  • You're an owner-occupier (private landlords have separate rules)

What ECO4 typically covers

  • Mid-terrace EWI£6,000 – £10,000
  • Semi-detached EWI£7,500 – £11,000
  • End-of-terrace EWI£8,000 – £11,500
  • Detached property EWI£9,000 – £12,000

In many cases ECO4 fully covers the EWI cost for qualifying households. Where the grant doesn't cover the full amount, you pay only the balance.

GBIS in detail

Great British Insulation Scheme. Broader eligibility, smaller grants.

GBIS opens funding to households that don't qualify for ECO4. No income test. Eligibility based on property characteristics alone.

You may qualify for GBIS if:

  • Your property is in Council Tax bands A to D (England)
  • Your property has an EPC rating of D, E, F or G
  • You're an owner-occupier or eligible private tenant
  • The property is your primary residence

London has higher Council Tax bands on average, so a smaller proportion of London households qualify for GBIS than the national average. Most qualifying London households are in inner-suburban locations.

What GBIS typically covers

  • Mid-terrace EWI£4,000 – £7,000
  • Semi-detached EWI£5,000 – £8,000
  • End-of-terrace EWI£5,500 – £8,500
  • Detached property EWI£6,000 – £9,000

GBIS typically covers half to two-thirds of the cost. You pay the balance directly.
