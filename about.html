About

London's specialist external wall insulation company.

External wall insulation has a trust problem. We exist to do it properly. Certified delivery, transparent fixed pricing, a single team from first call to final sign-off, and a government-endorsed quality framework throughout.

Why we exist

External wall insulation transforms homes. A solid-wall Victorian terrace insulated properly retains heat dramatically better, stays cooler through summer heatwaves, costs less to run year-round, sounds quieter inside, and is worth more on sale. Done well, EWI is one of the highest-impact upgrades available to a London homeowner.

The problem is that it isn't always done well. The EWI industry, especially in its grant-funded segments, has been damaged by a long tail of cowboy operators: door-to-door salespeople with unrealistic promises, sub-contractors with no accreditation, jobs walked away from mid-installation, and homeowners left out of pocket. The story of post-2010 retrofit programmes is littered with these cases.

The result is a customer who's heard horror stories before they even start researching. Even legitimate installers struggle to win trust because the category itself has a credibility problem.

We exist to do EWI properly. Rigorous standards, transparent fixed pricing, certified delivery, and a single accountable team from your first enquiry through to aftercare twelve months after the scaffold comes down.

How we work

Every project follows the same structured PAS 2035 retrofit pathway: free survey, fixed-price quote within 48 hours, grant administration handled on your behalf, and certified installation by our team. You deal with the same people throughout. No sub-contracting your enquiry to a third party, no handoffs between separate companies during installation, no being passed around when you need aftercare.

Our specialist focus on London's solid-wall housing means our surveyors and installers understand the technical specifics that catch out generalists: bay window detailing, party wall considerations, conservation area planning rules, period property mortar matching, tight access alleys, and the moisture management nuances of Victorian and Edwardian construction.

We carry the certifications, hold the warranties, and stand behind every installation in our own name. If anything needs resolving after the scaffold comes down, you call us directly.

The standards we deliver to

We hold ourselves to a defined set of standards, and only proceed on a project once each is in place:

  • TrustMark registration, the government-endorsed quality scheme for tradespeople and home improvement firms
  • PAS 2030 certification for external wall insulation, the UK installation standard required for any work funded by ECO or government schemes
  • Active PAS 2035 retrofit co-ordination on every project, providing independent oversight of specification and quality
  • Current contracts with obligated energy companies funding ECO4, which means grant applications are processed quickly and reliably
  • BBA-certified insulation systems with 25-year warranties registered in your name
  • Adequate insurance including public liability, employer's liability, and professional indemnity
  • Specialist knowledge of London period properties, with installations sequenced and detailed to suit Victorian and Edwardian construction

Why we focus on London specifically

London's housing stock is unlike anywhere else in the UK. Solid 9-inch brickwork is the norm rather than the exception. Bay windows, party walls, conservation considerations, tight access alleys, and planning sensitivities are present on most properties. Listed buildings, conservation areas, and locally listed buildings concentrate in inner boroughs.

The technical specifics of insulating a Battersea Victorian terrace are different from insulating a 1970s semi in the suburbs. The grant eligibility rules in London, where Council Tax bands skew higher, play out differently from regional averages.

We've focused on London because the technical and regulatory specifics demand specialist knowledge. Generalist national firms miss the detail that matters here.

Standards

The frameworks we operate within.

Quality assurance in EWI is built on a stack of government-endorsed standards. Here's what each one actually means.

  • TrustMark

    The only government-endorsed quality scheme for tradespeople in the home improvement sector. Provides independent dispute resolution, financial protection, and standards monitoring. Every installation we deliver is lodged with TrustMark.

  • PAS 2030:2019

    The UK installation standard for energy efficiency measures. Sets out detailed requirements for installer competence, on-site quality control, and documentation. Mandatory for any ECO4 or GBIS funded installation.

  • PAS 2035:2023

    The retrofit co-ordination standard. Requires an independent qualified retrofit co-ordinator on every project to oversee specification, design, and quality assurance. Adds a layer of independent oversight that didn't exist in earlier schemes.

  • BBA certification

    The British Board of Agrément independently tests and certifies construction products. Every insulation system we install carries BBA certification covering material performance, durability, and a 25-year system warranty.

  • ECO4 and Great British Insulation Scheme

    The two main government-funded schemes that pay for EWI installations. Funding is provided by the major UK energy companies. We hold current contracts with multiple obligated suppliers, which means applications are processed quickly and reliably.
