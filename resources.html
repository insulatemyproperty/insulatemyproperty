Featured guide

10 questions to ask any EWI installer.

The most important resource on this page. Print it out, take it to every quote conversation, and refuse to engage with anyone who can't answer all ten clearly.

  1. i.

    Are you TrustMark registered for external wall insulation?

    TrustMark is the only government-endorsed quality scheme for the home improvement sector. Anyone unable to show current TrustMark registration shouldn't be installing EWI on your home, full stop.

  2. ii.

    What's your PAS 2030 certification and when was it last audited?

    PAS 2030 is the UK installation standard. Certification is annual and audited. Ask for the certificate number and check it on the certification body's website.

  3. iii.

    Who is the PAS 2035 retrofit co-ordinator on my project?

    PAS 2035 requires an independent retrofit co-ordinator on every project. If the installer doesn't have one in place, the project isn't compliant with current standards.

  4. iv.

    Which specific BBA-certified system will you install on my property?

    A specific system name (e.g. "Wetherby OPTIPRO with mineral wool") shows competence. Vague answers about "high-performance insulation" suggest the installer is choosing the cheapest available, not the best fit.

  5. v.

    Can I see three recent customer references in London?

    Genuine installers can provide local references easily. References from completely different regions or none at all is a serious warning.

  6. vi.

    Is the quoted price fixed, or could it change during installation?

    A genuine fixed-price quote is non-negotiable. "Subject to site conditions" wording without specifics often hides intent to bill more later.

  7. vii.

    How is the BBA warranty registered, and is it transferable on sale?

    A 25-year warranty registered in your name with the system manufacturer is standard. Installer-issued warranties (rather than system warranties) are weaker and disappear if the installer goes bust.

  8. viii.

    What's your process for damp and condensation risk assessment?

    A competent installer assesses moisture risk as part of the survey and specifies ventilation upgrades if needed. Skipping this is the most common cause of post-EWI damp problems.

  9. ix.

    If grants apply, which energy company is funding my project?

    A specific obligated supplier name shows the installer has live contracts. Generic answers about "ECO funding" without a named supplier suggest they may be sub-contracting their grant work.

  10. x.

    What insurance do you carry?

    Public liability (£5m minimum), employer's liability (£10m), and professional indemnity should all be in place. Ask to see current certificates. No insurance is no deal.
