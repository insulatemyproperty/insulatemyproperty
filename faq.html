Frequently asked

The questions people actually ask.

Honest answers to the questions that come up before, during, and after an external wall insulation project.

About the product

What is external wall insulation?

External wall insulation (EWI) is an insulation system applied to the outside of your existing walls, behind a new render or brick-effect finish. The system typically comprises insulation boards (mineral wool, EPS, phenolic foam, or wood fibre), mechanical fixings and adhesive, a reinforcing mesh, and a render finish. The result is a continuous thermal layer that dramatically reduces heat loss through the walls.

Will it damage my brickwork?

No. EWI is fixed to the exterior of your existing wall using mechanical fixings and adhesive specified for your wall type. The original brickwork is unaffected, and the system is fully reversible if ever required. Heritage and conservation-grade systems exist for sensitive properties.

Can it cause damp issues?

Properly specified and installed EWI reduces damp risk by raising internal wall temperatures and eliminating cold spots where moisture condenses. Damp problems associated with EWI typically result from poorly specified systems or skipped ventilation upgrades. Our PAS 2035 co-ordination process specifically addresses moisture management at design stage.

What about my bay window?

Bay windows require careful detailing to maintain weathering, reveals, and original proportions. Our installers specify bespoke detailing for each bay configuration. Period properties with ornate bay windows can be insulated without losing the character of the elevation.

Will the house look different afterward?

Yes, to some degree. Your walls will be roughly 100mm thicker, the finish will be render or brick-effect cladding rather than the original brick, and reveals around windows will be deeper. Many homeowners see this as a positive (a fresh, well-maintained appearance); for others, especially on character properties, we recommend brick slip cladding or heritage finishes that closely match the original.

Does EWI help in summer too, or only winter?

It helps significantly in summer. The same insulating layer that traps heat indoors during winter blocks solar heat gain through the walls during summer. UK heatwaves are getting more frequent and inner-London terraces are particularly prone to overheating; EWI typically reduces peak internal temperatures by 3 to 5°C during hot weather, and slows the rate at which heat builds up through the day. For homeowners who currently rely on fans or portable air conditioning in July and August, the difference is noticeable in the first summer after installation.

About cost and grants

How much does external wall insulation cost?

Headline costs range from £10,000 to £22,000 depending on property type and specification. After typical grant funding for eligible households, net costs are £6,500 to £14,000. See our pricing page for detailed packages and our grants page for funding amounts.

Do I qualify for a grant?

Many London households qualify for at least partial funding. ECO4 prioritises lower-income households on qualifying benefits and homes with poor EPC ratings. GBIS is broader, covering Council Tax bands A to D with EPC ratings D to G. Our survey confirms exactly what you qualify for; check the grants page for full eligibility detail.

What if I don't qualify for a grant?

You can self-fund the installation in full. The headline cost is still recouped through energy savings over time, and EWI typically adds to property valuation by more than its installed cost. Many homeowners also choose to combine EWI with other planned external works (new roof, repointing, render renewal) for a single scaffold and cost-efficient sequencing.

How much will it save on my energy bills?

For a typical solid-wall Victorian terrace, EWI reduces heating costs by 30 to 50 percent. In cash terms that's roughly £900 to £1,400 per year off gas bills at current energy prices. The exact figure depends on current consumption, system specification, and how the property is used.

How do you make money?

We charge a fixed price for the installation that covers materials, labour, scaffolding, survey, grant administration, warranties, and aftercare. We don't markup grant funding. We don't charge for surveys or quotes. Our pricing is transparent and competitive, with the benefit of certified delivery and a single point of accountability.

About the install

Who actually does the work?

We do. We hold TrustMark registration, PAS 2030 certification for external wall insulation, current ECO4 energy company contracts, and BBA-certified system specifications. Every project includes PAS 2035 retrofit co-ordination providing independent oversight. The same team handles your enquiry, survey, install, and aftercare.

How long does the install take?

A typical 2 to 3 bed terraced property takes 2 to 4 weeks on site, scaffold up to scaffold down. Larger properties or those needing remedial work take longer. Weather can extend timelines slightly in winter. You'll have a clear schedule before work begins.

Do I need planning permission?

EWI is generally permitted development in most London boroughs, but exceptions apply for listed buildings, conservation areas, and properties facing the highway. Our survey identifies any planning requirements and we'll guide you through any necessary applications before quoting.

Do I need to be at home during installation?

No. The work is external. You don't need to be home during the day, although our installer will need access to your meter, water supply, and outdoor power for tools. We agree practical access arrangements before installation begins.

What about my neighbours?

Scaffolding usually sits within your own property boundary, but party-wall and shared-access considerations apply for many London properties. Where access through a neighbour's property is needed, we help facilitate the conversation and any necessary party wall agreements.

What happens if you find problems during the survey?

Common issues include existing damp, render failure, structural cracking, or failing pointing. We flag these in the survey report along with recommendations and indicative costs. You can choose to address them as part of the EWI project or independently, but they don't get hidden or ignored.

After installation

How long does the warranty last?

The BBA system warranty covers the insulation system for 25 years. It's registered in your name with the system manufacturer and is fully transferable if you sell the property. Workmanship is separately guaranteed by us for 12 to 24 months depending on system.

What if something goes wrong after install?

You contact us directly. We handle any aftercare in-house. If the issue is covered under the BBA warranty, the system manufacturer is the warranty holder and we manage that process on your behalf. If we're ever unable to resolve a dispute directly, TrustMark provides independent dispute resolution at no cost to you.

Will I need a new EPC certificate?

Yes. A new EPC is issued post-installation reflecting the improved thermal performance of your property. EWI typically improves an EPC rating by one to two bands, which has implications for property valuation and any future grant eligibility.

What if I sell the property?

The 25-year BBA system warranty is transferable to subsequent owners. The improved EPC rating supports valuation. The full project documentation pack we provide makes the upgrade easy to evidence at sale.
